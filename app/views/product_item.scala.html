I love the double manual. It's nice to see its iOS and Android compatible. (Not 100% mandatory but typically there's a Spanish manuel included.

Josh Abdul

The packaging might need some visual treatment. If I were to pick this up off of the self from a store I would really know what I was getting. There's no information in regards to what wattage the lights should be used with, what devices its compatable with, what comes in the box, company website info, etc.

You can't control your lights while the music is streaming. Not a big deal, but the app doesn't give any feedback about the wifi lights status. For example; if music is playing and my only options are to dim the light or turn it off the other light functions should display as disabled or the app should alert me the bulb is busy streaming music.

The QBulb app works great, but might need some more instructions. After downloading the app for the 1st time I landed on the BellQ welcome screen. There are 3 options to choose from. A wifi icon, a cinema/movie icon, and a bulb icon. I selected the bulb icon like the instructions stated, but it would be even more clear if the icons had labels. (I did a test with my friend to see how fast he could set the bulb up without instructions. He figured everything out for the most part, but when he got to the app he didn't know what selection to make.)

This might be nitpicky, but I feel like it makes more sense for the color mode/white light mode to be a tab or toggle option. Very similar to what you would see at the top of the "top charts" section of the iTunes Store App [ Paid | Free | Top Grossing ]. The only reason I say that is because it dosnt make sense to have a color wheel on white light mode.

The instructions didn't discribe how to differentiate light A B and C from one another. Is light A the first light a consumer screws in and turns on? Does the user have to turn the bulbs on in a sequential order to determine which light is what?

It's not 100% clear on how to get back to the Home screen from the bulb control page. I always have to shutdown then restart the app to get to the rest of the music and wifi settings.

I found it weird that the divice settings screens weren't designed to the iPhones screen size.

On the Kickstarter video I saw there was a way to sync your QBulb to your alarm clock. It might be a good idea to include the how to guide in the manual or direct the user to some tips and tricks on the website. (Instructional Youtubes have been very popular and helpful.
